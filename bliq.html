Bliq

Bliq is an all-in-one payment companion. Easily store all your cards, bank accounts, and digital wallets in one secure place — no more switching apps or digging through your bag. Whether you’re shopping online, paying in-store, or splitting a bill with friends, Bliq makes every transaction fast, smooth, and stress-free. With top-level security and an interface designed for simplicity, Bliq puts you in full control of your money — anytime, anywhere.

Tools: Figma, Illustrator

Timeline: 6 months

THE DESIGN THINKING PROCESS

UNDERSTAND

“In order to solve a problem, you need to understand a problem.” This means that the first step will be analyzing the current market competitors and what they offer.

Through a competitive analysis, I will be able to find out information about the strongest competitors in the current market, what marketing strategy they use to attract customers, and customer support options since nowadays many people rely on online support and also don’t have the time or other circumstances to go ...

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Users need a way to make secure transactions and keep track of their spending because the increasing use of online banking and phone payments exposes them to a higher risk of scams and fraud.​

We will know this to be true when we see a reduction of fraud reports, and a rise of new users due to the words of safe banking spreading around.

COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

Analyzing two major competitors provides valuable insights into current market, user expectations, and industry standards, helping to guide the overall design strategy.

Overview HSBC:

Key Objectives:

HSBC emphasizes its global facilitator role (meaning that they also speak many different languages), creating opportunities and enabling progress for individuals, companies, and institutions worldwide. As one of the biggest banks worldwide, they brand themselves as a reliable, globally connected, and innovative financial partner.

Overall Strategy:

HSBC attracts its potential customers with their global reach, diverse services, focus on sustainability, focus on safe digital banking, and maintaining leadership in the market.

Market Advantage

HSBC invests heavily in technology to allow advanced digital banking through mobile apps, online banking, AI services, and in general to make it more accessible. They also offer a 24/7 support line and are highly chosen due to their good service and competitive interest rates.

Conclusion

HSBC’s strategy highlights the importance of positioning a financial service as not only secure and innovative, but also globally accessible and customer-centric. Their focus on digital banking, multilingual support, and around-the-clock assistance demonstrates how trust, accessibility, and convenience can become strong market advantages.

How could this help influence my app:
  • Accessibility: Offering multilingual options or easy-to-understand communication can broaden the user base.
  • Trust & Security: Placing emphasis on safe digital banking builds confidence, especially for users new or generally skeptical of financial apps.
  • Customer-Centric Features: Features like instant support, intuitive navigation, and seamless digital tools can make the app seem more reliable and attractive.

Overview PayPal:

Key Objectives:

PayPal is a global platform available in over 200 countries, supporting numerous currencies to facilitate easy and secure transactions worldwide, whether for personal use, e-commerce, or marketplaces. Renowned for its robust security measures that prevent fraud, PayPal has built a strong reputation for reliability and trustworthiness.

Overall Strategy:

PayPal attracts customers with fast transfers, user-friendly app and website accessibility, and robust buyer protection, addressing concerns about online security. Their slogan, "Put People First, Work Customer Back, and Win Together," emphasizes developing potential, providing feedback, and focusing on customers' needs through tech innovation. They embrace change, act quickly, collaborate, and deliver outstanding results.

Market Advantage

PayPal's market advantages include its global reach, supporting over 200 countries and many currencies, and strong security measures that build trust among users. Its user-friendly experience and quick transfer capabilities set it apart from traditional banks. With versatile payment options and mobile accessibility, including the Venmo app, PayPal meets a variety of customer needs.

Conclusion

PayPal’s success is rooted in its ability to combine global accessibility with a seamless and secure user experience. By focusing on speed, ease of use, and strong buyer protection, PayPal positions itself as a trusted digital payment leader worldwide. Its emphasis on customer-first values, rapid adaptation, and continuous innovation reinforces its reputation as a platform built around reliability and user needs.

How could this help influence my app:
  • Seamless User Experience: A simple, intuitive design that makes transactions fast and hassle-free keeps users engaged.
  • Security as a Core Value: Emphasizing fraud prevention and digital safety helps build long-term trust.
  • Global & Flexible Reach: Supporting multiple currencies or flexible payment options can expand your app’s usability across diverse markets.
  • Customer-Centric Growth: Like PayPal, keeping users at the center of development ensures your app evolves to meet real needs.

OBSERVE

This phase is all about collecting information about customers´ needs, goals, and complaints.

How satisfied are they with their current banking apps? How long have they been there? What made them decide for this one? What do they like about it? What do they not like about it? How safe do they feel when paying online? Are they open to new banking apps?

All those questions will be answered by conducting user interviews in public spaces and online surveys.

RESEARCH GOALS

Analyzing User Behavior & Banking Habits

Gaining a clearer understanding of how interviewees currently interact with their banking apps, including which app they use, how frequently they use it, and the specific features they engage with most often.

Analyzing User Behavior & Banking Habits

Acquiring a deeper insight into the aspects users dislike or find frustrating in their current banking apps, identifying areas for improvement, and uncovering additional features they wish were available to enhance their overall experience.

Identifying User Concerns & Long-Term Retention

Gaining better insights into the growing concerns users have regarding the increasing threats of theft and scams in today’s digital landscape, as well as what factors influence their loyalty to their current bank or what features might compel them to switch to a different one.

INTERVIEW

Interviewees:

Data Collection Methods

To collect the data I used the user interview method. It is a foundational attitudinal method of user research, offering a straightforward, qualitative approach to understanding a product from the user’s perspective. Its greatest advantage is that it’s a cost-effective way to gather insights directly from the people who will be using your product.

However, one potential downside is that poorly phrased questions can introduce bias, making the interview less objective.

AFFINITY MAP

After finishing the user interviews I decided to create an affinity map as a way to:

Identify patterns – by organizing similar insights and statements to make sense of a large amount of data

Prioritize pain points and needs – by highlighting the issues that appeared most frequently

KEY INSIGHTS OF AFFINITY MAP

App Usage & Experience

  • Most users check their banking apps frequently, with daily or multiple times a week usage.
  • User experience opinions vary—some find their apps intuitive (Monzo, Apple Pay), while others struggle with complexity (Bawag).
  • Long-term users tend to stick with their bank due to familiarity rather than app satisfaction.

Pain Points & Desired Improvments

  • Some users feel overwhelmed or confused by certain app functionalities (e.g., Bawag).
  • Login and authentication issues are a recurring problem.
  • Some users want additional features like better financial insights or improved UI for certain banks.

Trust & Security

  • Security is a priority, and most users feel safe using their current apps
  • Few have experienced fraud, but some have had minor issues (e.g., accidental charges)

Loyalty & Willingness to Switch

  • Many users stay with their banking app due to habit, familiarity, or bank reputation
  • Some are open to switching, but only if the new app offers better benefits (e.g., higher savings rates, better support, improved user experience)
  • The biggest barrier to switching is the effort of learning a new systems

USER PERSONA(S)

Based on the user interview results, I created two contrasting user personas.

The first is a young, tech-savvy woman who navigates technology with ease but faces challenges due to a language barrier, which makes app usage more difficult despite her technical confidence.

The second is a middle-aged man who is fluent in the language but less confident with technology. While he sometimes struggles with digital tools, he is motivated to keep up and continue accomplishing the tasks he needs or wants to do.

USABILITY TESTING

Now that the high-fidelity prototype is done, it is time to test the work. For this I conducted a usability test with 5 participants.

Participants

The test will include five participants. Since the app is designed to be inclusive and accessible for all age groups, the only requirement is that participants have experience with online banking. If they already use neo-banks, that’s a plus.

Goal of testing

The goal of these sessions is to identify any steps that may be confusing, cause hesitation, or are more complex than necessary. Observing how users interact with the product will help us uncover issues we may have previously overlooked.

Goal of testing

  • General user-friendliness of the app
  • How easy it is to use the Exchange Currency feature
  • If the Onboarding is easy to find and easy to use without too much information
  • Other issues discovered during testing

Methodology

The moderated remote tests will be held online over Teams.

Session

Each session will last approximately 15–20 minutes, but I will allocate around 30 minutes per participant to allow time for the introduction and technical setup to ensure everything runs smoothly.

Schedule

Finding participants: 25.-28.04.2025

Conducting the test: 28.04-04.05.2025

TASKS

Task 1: Explore features

Scenario:

You open the app, but you're not sure how to find the options or tools you might need. What would you do next?​​

Post Task Questions:
  • Was the Explore feature option easy to find?
  • Do you think the way it was explained was clear and understandable?
  • What do you think of the length of the onboarding?
  • Do you have any feedback on what the onboarding might be missing?

Task 2: Finance Analysis

Scenario:

You want to get a better overview of how you're spending your money lately. What would you do?

Post Task Questions:
  • Did you automatically think of the Financial analysis feature?
  • Did you also consider just checking the recent transaction history?
  • How did you feel when analysing it through the graphs?
  • Is there something that was hard to understand?

Task 3: Exchanging currency

Scenario:

You’re planning a trip abroad and need to get some foreign currency. What would you do in the app?

Post Task Questions:
  • Was the Exchange currency option easy to find?
  • Do you have something that we could add/change?

Top 3 Issues with Severity

Issue 1: Confusion with login (High severity)

When logging in it came to some participants feeling lost as some of them don´t know their account number.

Suggested change:

Changing it from account number to email.

Issue 2: Difficulty seeing icons of the menu bar (High severity)

One out of six participants had significant difficulty understanding the menu icons, noting that the inactive icons were too light in grey, making them hard to distinguish. Additionally, three out of six participants suggested that increasing the visibility of these icons would improve usability.

Suggested change:

Adjust colours of Income and Expenses and check if everything matches the colour scheme and doesn´t stand out.

Issue 3: Colour scheme doesn´t match (Medium severity)

One out of six participants mentioned that she felt the colour scheme was occasionally inconsistent or unfitting. She pointed to the Income and Expenses feature as the most notable example.

Suggested change:

Making the icons more visible; either darker or adding a border.

CONCLUSION

This project has been a comprehensive journey that allowed me to expand both my creative and analytical skills. From the initial research phase I learned how to frame the right questions, conduct meaningful interviews, and translate raw data into actionable insights. Summarizing findings and applying them to design decisions strengthened my ability to think critically and empathize with users.

The design process itself taught me the value of iteration, moving from low-fidelity sketches to high-fidelity prototypes while continuously testing, refining, and adapting ideas. I experienced firsthand how much of the design work happens “behind the scenes” and how setbacks often lead to stronger, more thoughtful solutions.

Beyond the technical skills, this project enhanced my understanding of the full design lifecycle, including problem-solving, attention to detail, and balancing user needs with practical constraints. It also deepened my appreciation for collaboration, feedback, and adaptability which are essential qualities for creating meaningful digital products.

Ultimately, this experience not only strengthened my UX expertise but also gave me the confidence to bring an idea to life. I can proudly say that I have designed my own app, combining research, design, and testing into a product that reflects both user needs and my growth as a designer.
